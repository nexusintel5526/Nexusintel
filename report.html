Executive Summary

The Shift From Tools to Agents Is Happening Faster Than Anyone Predicted

In Q1 2026, the number of enterprise deployments featuring autonomous AI agents — systems that plan, execute multi-step tasks, and interact with external tools without continuous human oversight — grew by 340% year-over-year. This is not a gradual adoption curve. It is a step change.

The implications extend far beyond productivity gains. Agentic AI is triggering a fundamental reorganization of how work is structured, what skills are valued, and which companies will control critical infrastructure over the next decade. Early movers are already establishing moats that will be difficult to close.

"The question for enterprise leaders is no longer whether to deploy AI agents. It is whether they will build the institutional knowledge to direct them — or cede that capability to vendors."

Section 1 — Market Landscape (Preview)

The Three-Layer Stack That Will Define the Agentic Era

To understand where value will accrue in the agentic AI market, it helps to think in layers. The foundation layer — frontier models from Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and a handful of others — is becoming increasingly commoditized as capabilities converge. Differentiation at this layer is narrowing.

The orchestration layer — frameworks and platforms that coordinate multiple agents, manage memory, and handle tool access — is where the most consequential competition is currently playing out. This is the layer that determines which agents work together, how they share context, and who controls the guardrails.

The application layer sits atop the stack and is where most enterprise spending currently flows. However, our analysis of 847 enterprise deployments reveals a pattern that fundamentally challenges the prevailing assumption that application-layer solutions will capture the majority of long-term value. In fact, the data points strongly in the opposite direction — and the companies that understand this distinction are already repositioning their vendor strategies accordingly.

Section 2 of this report examines the 14 enterprise sectors in detail, mapping current adoption curves, regulatory constraints, and the specific workflow categories where agentic AI is generating measurable ROI versus where deployments are failing. Section 3 presents our proprietary framework for evaluating orchestration vendors — the make-or-buy decision that will define enterprise AI strategy for the next five years.

Our analysis of the labor market impact draws on proprietary survey data from 2,400 knowledge workers across six countries, supplemented by job posting analysis across 18 months. The findings challenge both the alarmist and dismissive narratives that currently dominate public discourse.
